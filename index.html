A Brief Bio

Prophit See is an animated character in his mid-30s, easily recognized by his loose white shirt, light-grey trousers, and a Nigerian-flag-themed headscarf—sometimes tied in Tupac’s style. His expression is usually calm, tired, or slightly dissatisfied, as if he’s constantly observing a world that isn’t living up to its potential. He rarely smiles fully, but when he does, it’s sincere and disarming.

Although he looks unhappy, Prophit is not defeated. Beneath that weary face is a strong will, an unshakable sense of humor, and a quiet optimism. He sees the problems in his community—misinformation, neglect, everyday struggles—and they bother him deeply. But instead of becoming bitter, Prophit transforms his frustration into clever jokes, dry humor, and playful storytelling that make people laugh while gently nudging them to think.

Prophit believes that real change doesn’t always come from anger; sometimes it comes from making people see things differently, using humor as a mirror. His goal is simple but powerful: help his community learn, reflect, and grow—using entertainment as his tool and truth as his guide.
